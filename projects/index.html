Extracting features from medical images

Self-supervised representation learning on Whole Slide Images

Medical images are hard to work with: they are big, strange, and there's a shortage of labeled datasets. As such, traditional feature extraction done with transfer learning from pretrained CNNs is problematic.

In this study, I contributed in researching representative SSL methodologies, as well as in building a custom method (VaE), and benchmark representational capabilities against ImageNet pretrained CNNs with a linear cancer-classification downstream task (an SVM fitted on embeddings).

Activation Shaping for domain adaptation

Is that still a cat?

We are pretty good at learning models ables to classify images. There's either a dog or a cat, no doubt about that. What about SKETCHED dogs though? and ART PAINTED cats? Domain adaptation is a hot topic in computer vision, we want models that are able to classify objects even if their domain is different from the training one.

In this work, I contributed in defining Activation Shaping strategies in order to enhance generalization capabilities of a pretrained Resnet18.
